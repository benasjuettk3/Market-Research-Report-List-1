<p><p>The knee reconstruction market comprises several implant types tailored to specific surgical needs. Cemented implants utilize bone cement for stability and are commonly used in primary surgeries. Cementless implants facilitate bone ingrowth, providing a more natural approach for long-term stability. Partial implants target damaged areas, preserving healthy tissue for less invasive procedures. Revision implants are designed for patients needing replacement after previous surgeries, addressing complications or implant failures. Each type caters to distinct clinical requirements, enhancing patient outcomes.</p></p>
