<p><p>The Drug-Eluting Balloon (DEB) market consists of two primary types: Type I and Type II. Type I refers to drugs that are coated on the balloon's surface and released upon inflation, providing localized treatment during the procedure. In contrast, Type II involves balloons that incorporate drugs into their matrix, allowing for a controlled and sustained release over time. These distinctions influence clinical applications, regulatory pathways, and market dynamics, with each type catering to different patient needs and vascular conditions.</p></p>
