<p><p>The photoelectrochemical cell market comprises various types, including dye-sensitized photovoltaic cells, photoelectrolytic cells, and others. Dye-sensitized cells harness light to generate electricity through a dye that absorbs photons, enhancing efficiency. Photoelectrolytic cells, on the other hand, facilitate water splitting to produce hydrogen fuel using light energy. Other variations include organic photoelectrochemical cells and tandem cells, which utilize multiple materials and processes to improve conversion rates and expand applications in renewable energy. Together, these technologies drive advancements in clean energy solutions.</p></p>
