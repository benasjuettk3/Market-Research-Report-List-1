<p><p>The Cross Linked Sodium Carboxymethyl Cellulose (CMC) market is categorized primarily into cotton-origin and wood-origin types. Cotton-origin CMC is derived from the natural cellulose found in cotton fibers, offering higher purity and better gel-forming properties, making it suitable for pharmaceuticals and food applications. Conversely, wood-origin CMC is sourced from cellulose in wood pulp, generally more cost-effective and widely used in industrial applications like paper manufacturing and textile treatment. Both types cater to diverse industries based on their properties and sourcing.</p></p>
