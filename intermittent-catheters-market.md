<p><p>Intermittent catheters are medical devices designed for the periodic drainage of urine from the bladder, primarily used by individuals with urinary retention issues or conditions that affect bladder function. These catheters promote better urinary health and reduce the risk of urinary tract infections (UTIs) compared to indwelling catheters. The market for intermittent catheters is experiencing significant growth due to an increasing prevalence of urinary disorders, the rising aging population, and the growing awareness of self-catheterization among patients.</p><p>Recent trends include the development of advanced materials and designs, such as hydrophilic-coated catheters that enhance comfort and ease of use. Additionally, the shift towards outpatient care and home healthcare solutions is contributing to the market expansion.
